Foods Not For Lactating Mom

Breast milk is a superb combination of proteins, fats, vitamins, and carbohydrates as well as the antibodies providing the baby the necessary nutrition and immunity.

However to keep the baby safe from any kind of food borne poisoning or illness, every breastfeeding moms including you need to avoid or limit the following foods:

  • Avoid seafood that’s high in mercury, including shark, swordfish, king mackerel and tilefish to limit your baby’s exposure to mercury.
  • If you eat fish from local waters, make sure the source is free from industrial chemicals. Limit their intake not more than 6 ounces (170 grams) per week.
  • Limit saturated fats in high-fat meats, whole milk, tropical oils (coconut), butter, lard and avoid trans fats in partially hydrogenated oils as both of these are unhealthy for your and your baby.
  • Avoid water that is not filtered. Filtration removes the metals, if there is any, in the supply water.
  • Avoid drinking more than 2 to 3 cups of caffeinated drinks such as coffee, tea, soft drinks, energy drinks, chocolate, and coffee ice cream per day as too much caffeine can interfere with your baby’s sleep or make him fussy.
  • If your baby becomes irritable or have an allergic reaction, better eliminate dairy products or other allergic foods or ingredients, such as: egg, peanuts, tree nuts, wheat, cow’s milk.
  • Breastfeeding moms should avoid or limit as much as possible fruits and vegetables rich in pesticides. Apples, celery, strawberries, peaches, spinach, imported grapes, sweet bell peppers, potatoes, blueberries, lettuce, kale and collard greens were tested highest for pesticides as reported by Environmental Working Group. Always wash 5-6 times fruits and veggies well, peel the skin of fruits.
  • Don’t get more than 2,500 mg daily calcium from all sources. Exceeding this safe upper limit can lead to kidney stones, hypercalcemia, and renal insufficiency syndrome. It can also interfere with your body’s absorption of iron, magnesium, phosphorus, and zinc.
  • A big no to alcohol.
  • If your baby appears uncomfortable after you eat a particular food: check whether it occurs every time or not. If yes, avoid it to see if your baby is happier or not. In rare instances, your baby may be allergic to something you’ve eaten. If this is the case, you may notice a reaction on his skin (rash or hives), in his breathing (wheezing or congestion), or in his stools (green in color or have mucus). Breastfeeding moms should avoid those foods too.
